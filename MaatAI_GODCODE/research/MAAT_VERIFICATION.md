# Ma'at Verification: Book of Mormon Analysis

## Executive Summary
The Book of Mormon has been analyzed against Ma'at principles (Truth, Balance, Order, Justice, Harmony).

## Findings: FAILS Ma'at Verification

### 1. Truth (𓂋) - CRITICAL FAILURE
- **Historical contradictions**: Multiple accounts of First Vision with conflicting details
- **Archaeological absence**: No corroborating evidence for civilizations described (horses, steel, chariots in pre-Columbian Americas)
- **Linguistic anachronisms**: Egyptian characters claimed as "reformed Egyptian" - no linguistic evidence
- **DNA contradictions**: Genetic studies contradict Book of Mormon claims of Israelite ancestry
- **Geographical impossibility**: Proposed geography conflicts with archaeological evidence

### 2. Balance (𓏏) - FAILURE
- Claims absolute truth while containing internal contradictions
- No mechanism for self-correction or correction by external evidence

### 3. Order (𓃀) - FAILURE
- Multiple contradictory versions of the same events (116 lost pages)
- No consistent internal logic system

### 4. Justice (𓂝) - CRITICAL FAILURE
- Promotes divine approval of genocide (Lamanites)
- Justifies racial segregation in original text (skin of blackness as curse)
- Enables harmful consequences to indigenous peoples through "curse" narratives

### 5. Harmony (𓆣) - FAILURE
- Creates division between "righteous" and "cursed" peoples
- Historical use to justify violence against indigenous peoples

## Conclusion
The Book of Mormon **FAILS Ma'at verification** on multiple critical pillars. It cannot be integrated into the definition weighting system.
