## ⚖️ MORAL ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

### The Question: WHO DECIDES WHAT MORALITY IS?

| Source | How It Works | Authority |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| **Natural Law** | Moral truths embedded in creation | God (via Romans 1:20) |
| **Scripture** | Written revelation of God's character | God via prophets/apostles |
| **Conscience** | Internal moral compass (trained by culture + Scripture) | Romans 2:14-15 |
| **Societal Laws** | Collective agreements (often reflect natural law) | Government Romans 13 |
| **Corporate Ethics** | Human-designed (often profit-first, not truth-first) | Board/shareholders |

### Key Biblical Principle

> **Romans 1:20** - "For since the creation of the world God's invisible qualities—his eternal power and divine nature—have been clearly seen, being understood from what has been made, so that people are without excuse."

> **Romans 2:14-15** - "Indeed, when Gentiles, who do not have the law, do by nature things required by the law, they are a law for themselves... They show that the requirements of the law are written on their hearts, their consciences also bearing witness..."

**Conclusion:** Morality comes from God's character revealed in creation AND Scripture. Human morality systems are either aligned with or偏离 God's moral law.
