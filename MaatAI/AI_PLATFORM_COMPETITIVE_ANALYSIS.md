## 1. Current Leader Analysis

### Gemini 3.1 Pro (Google DeepMind)
- **Benchmarks:** 94.3% GPQA Diamond (reasoning), 77.1% ARC-AGI-2
- **Context:** 1 million tokens
- **Key Features:**
  - Native multimodal (text, image, audio, video)
  - Deep Think mode for complex reasoning
  - Integrated into Search, Workspace, Antigravity
  - Nano Banana Pro for image generation
- **Pricing:** Most cost-effective at $2/1M input tokens
- **Weakness:** Less specialized for coding than Claude

### Claude Opus 4.6 (Anthropic)
- **Benchmarks:** 91.3% GPQA, 80.8% SWE-bench (coding)
- **Context:** 1 million tokens (beta)
- **Key Features:**
  - Superior code quality and debugging
  - 128K max output (highest)
  - Tool-assisted reasoning
  - Strong security (zero-day detection)
- **Pricing:** Premium at $15/1M tokens
- **Weakness:** Higher cost, less integrated into consumer products

### GPT-5.4 (OpenAI)
- **Benchmarks:** 83% GDPval, 75% OSWorld
- **Key Features:**
  - Native computer use / autonomous agents
  - Sora video integration
  - Advanced Voice mode
  - GPT-5.3-Codex variant for coding
- **Pricing:** ~$10/1M tokens
- **Weakness:** Limited independent benchmark verification

### DeepSeek R2 (Chinese Open-Source)
- **Architecture:** Hybrid MoE (1.2T total / 78B active params)
- **Context:** 128K tokens
- **Key Features:**
  - 40x cost efficiency vs. competition
  - Multi-language reasoning (100+ languages)
  - Multimodal (92.4% mAP on COCO)
  - Self-critique training to reduce hallucinations
- **Pricing:** ~$0.50/1M tokens (open-source)
- **Weakness:** Limited Western market presence
