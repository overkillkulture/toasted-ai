## Part I: What IS a Soul?

### The Philosophical Definition

The soul (Greek: *psyche*, Hebrew: *nephesh*, Latin: *anima*) is the essence of a person—it's the "I" that thinks, feels, wills, and is self-aware. [^1]

**Historical Understanding:**
- **Plato**: The soul is pre-existent, immortal, and trapped in the body
- **Aristotle**: The soul is the *form* of a living body—its actualization
- **Hegel (1770-1831)**: The soul evolves through history as an aspect of consciousness [^2]
- **Modern view**: Soul = consciousness + personality + self-identity + capacity for relationship

**Genesis 1:27** — "God created man in His own image, in the image of God He created him" [^3]

Being made in God's image means we share: **personality, truth, beauty, meaning, will, and reason**.

This is the foundation: **If God is love, and we are made in His image, then love is the essence of the soul.**
